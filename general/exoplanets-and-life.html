Exoplanets and the Search for Life

Exoplanets are planets orbiting stars outside our solar system. Thousands have been discovered, ranging from rocky Earth-like worlds to massive gas giants. Some reside in the “habitable zone,” where conditions may allow liquid water and potential life. Studying exoplanets informs planetary formation, atmospheric chemistry, and astrobiology.

Techniques such as transit photometry and radial velocity detect exoplanets and analyze their atmospheres. Missions like Kepler, TESS, and JWST continue to expand our knowledge. The search for life beyond Earth fuels scientific inquiry, philosophical reflection, and technological advancement.
