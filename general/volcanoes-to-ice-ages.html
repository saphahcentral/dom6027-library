Earth’s Geological History: From Volcanoes to Ice Ages

Earth’s geological history spans billions of years, shaped by volcanic activity, plate tectonics, erosion, and climate changes. Volcanoes formed continents and islands, while earthquakes and shifting plates reshaped landscapes. Ice ages created glaciers that sculpted valleys and transported rocks over vast distances. Sedimentary layers preserve fossils, revealing ancient life and environmental conditions.

Studying Earth’s geological past helps scientists predict natural hazards, locate mineral resources, and understand climate evolution. For researchers, the planet’s history is a record of dynamic forces and life’s resilience. By examining rocks, fossils, and formations, humanity gains insight into the processes that shaped the world we inhabit today.
