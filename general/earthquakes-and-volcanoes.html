Earthquakes and Volcanoes: Forces of Nature

Earthquakes and volcanoes are powerful geological processes that shape landscapes and influence life on Earth. Earthquakes occur due to sudden movements along fault lines, releasing energy in the form of seismic waves. Volcanoes form where magma reaches the surface, creating mountains, islands, and lava plains. Both phenomena can cause devastation, yet they also play a role in forming fertile soils and mineral deposits.
