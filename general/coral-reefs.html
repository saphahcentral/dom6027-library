Coral Reefs: Marine Biodiversity Hotspots

Coral reefs are among the most diverse ecosystems on Earth. Found in shallow, warm waters, they host thousands of species of fish, invertebrates, and plants. Coral polyps build calcium carbonate skeletons that create intricate reef structures, providing shelter, food, and breeding grounds for marine life.

Threats to coral reefs include climate change, ocean acidification, overfishing, and pollution. Conservation efforts aim to protect and restore these ecosystems, recognizing their importance for biodiversity, coastal protection, and livelihoods. Studying coral reefs enhances understanding of marine ecology and emphasizes humanity’s responsibility to preserve natural wonders.
