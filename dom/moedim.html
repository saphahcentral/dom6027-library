The Mo’edim – Appointed Times of YHVH

The word Mo’edim (מוֹעֲדִים) comes from the root ya’ad, meaning “appointed, designated, set apart.” In Beresith 1:14, YHVH declares that the sun, moon, and stars were placed in the heavens for “signs and for seasons (mo’edim), for days and for years.” These were never merely agricultural markers—they were divine appointments with humanity.

The Spring Mo’edim

  • Pesach (Passover) – Deliverance by the blood of the Lamb, fulfilled in Yahusha’s sacrifice.
  • Chag HaMatzot (Unleavened Bread) – A call to holiness, cleansing sin from our lives.
  • Yom HaBikkurim (First Fruits) – The resurrection of Yahusha, the first of those raised from the dead.
  • Shavuot (Weeks/Pentecost) – The giving of Torah at Sinai, and the giving of the Ruach HaQodesh in Yerushalayim.

The Fall Mo’edim

  • Yom Teru’ah (Trumpets) – A call to repentance, heralding the return of the King.
  • Yom Kippur (Day of Atonement) – A day of judgment and reconciliation with YHVH.
  • Sukkot (Tabernacles) – The joy of dwelling with YHVH, looking ahead to the Messianic Kingdom.

Mo’edim and Prophecy

Each Mo’ed is a prophetic rehearsal. The spring festivals point to the first coming of Yahusha, while the fall festivals point to His return and reign. Living by these Mo’edim aligns Derech Olam with the rhythm of heaven.

“These are the appointed feasts of YHVH, holy convocations, which you shall proclaim at their appointed time.” (Vayikra / Leviticus 23:4)

Conclusion

The Mo’edim are not “Jewish holidays” nor “obsolete rituals.” They are eternal appointments of YHVH, rooted in Torah and fulfilled in Yahusha. As Derech Olam Ministries, we embrace them as both memorials and prophetic rehearsals of the coming Kingdom. To walk in them is to walk in the light of the Tree of Life, rejecting the counterfeit calendars and systems of men.
